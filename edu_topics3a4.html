How to dress

When looking for something to wear for a interview people tend to forget what they should wear. Many people settle for their favorite rip jeans, and a white tee. However, before you say a single word to the interviewer, you have already made an impression based on how you're dressed... your first impresssion means a lot.

Listed below will be what Males and Females are expected to wear when they are going into a interview.

Male Job Attire

1. Always dress in a professionally appropriate manner
This doesn't mean you have to go to the Mens Warehouse and rent out a five-hundred follar suit. Think smart, sharp, and stylish
2. Make sure your clothes fit
No baggy pants; make sure everything is fitted well to your body.
3. Choose a dark colored suit
Avoid flashy colors !
4. Always do your laundry
Whatever you wear it needs to be clean and ironed, otherwise you're at risk of looking lazy and careless
5. Wear good quality, polished shoes
Pick a nice leather pair in black or brown shoes and give them a fresh coat of shoe polish

Female Job Attire

1. Always dress in a professionally appropriate manner
This doesn't mean you have to go on a shopping spree for one outfit. Pick something that you already have in your closet... just make sure it looks presentable.

Interview

You have finally gotten this far and this is the most important part of all. How you present yourself in a interview determines whether or not you will be getting the job... so you must take this seriously !

Listed below will be rules to follow while in a interview
1. Maintain eye contact- Doing this shows that you're interested and listening to what is being said by the interviewer. It also shows that you have confidence in what you're dicussing also.

2. Sit up straight- Good posture is always good to have, it demonstrates you have a strong sense of self-esteem. You should NEVER be slouched down in the chair !!!! You must lift your chin and push your shoulders back against the chair; with your feet flat on the floor.
**You might also want to cross your legs**

3. Pause before you speak- Rushing a answer may lead to you not fully getting your point across ! When you're being asked a question you should take a minute to brainstorm in your head how you will answer. This will gaurantee that you answer the question more throughly and confidently. Avoid repeating the words "UMM" and "Like" these words justify that you do not understand what you are trying to say also shows you do not have a strong vocabulary.

4. Be prepared for general interview questions- Always be ready to answer any question that may be thrown at you.

Interviewers love to ask questions such as...

Tell me about yourself. What are your strengths? What are your weaknesses?
Why do you want this job?
Where would you like to be in your career five years from now?
What's your ideal company?
What attracted you to this company?
Why should we hire you?
What did you like least about your last job?
When were you most satisfied in your job?
What can you do for us that other candidates can't?

5. Keep your answers clear and concise - Try not to drag your answers out a lot for questions. Many poeple tend to do this and the answer starts to slowly go away from the topic from which the question was asking. Keep it SHORT, SWEET, and to the POINT.

6. Get there early - You do not want to be the person that is running late for a interview. You will be automatically taking off the list for getting the job. If you're late for the interview that is disrespectful to the instution. It also lets the interview know that if you're late for the interview, you will also be late if you were hired. Take into consideration that this company has taking the time to have a person come sit with you and get to know you better and see if you're fit for the job... take advantage of thaty opporunity.
