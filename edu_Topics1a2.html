How To Search For Jobs !

Four main things to consider when searching...

1. The People

When trying to figure out where to work the first thing that comes to mind is the personailties of the pople that may work there. Everyone wants a team of co-workers that are nice,fun, and allow you to succeed in your job without any distractions. Although one should consider the people that may work at the institution DO NOT make that your MAIN FOCUS ! Everyone has different traits that make up who they are, and if they let that effect how you work that also effects whether or not you will still have a Job. The companies that hire poeple do not have time to baby you, and deal with issues that occur on the job site. If one has a problem they are held accountable to fix it. If that approach does not work then consider a job where there is little interaction with other employees. Futhermore,one starts to question the type of boss they will receive. Asking questions such as, Will my boss be strict? Will my boss be rude ? Would my boss allow me to have a flexible schedule ?

2. The Environment

Always pick a job that is a good fit for you. They're very, very different environments, and it's important to decide which you'd thrive in. If you're more of an individual worker who likes structure and competition, fit yourself into an institution that accommodates your needs. However, beware this goes for any Job your'e looking for... not just for the exanple metioned.

The physical location is also important to consider. A long commute from home or a lack of lunch options may pull down your everyday attitude. Nothing is worse than going to a miserable work enivronment every morning, then taken unhappiness home with you. In addition considering you'll be spending 40 hours a week (or more) in your work environment, it should be one that encourages callaboration.

3. The Benefits

Having a great benefits package is important for more than the obvious reasons. If the company offers it's employees perks like health, dental, retirement, and flexible spending plans, it can mean they're competitive and doing well finacially. However, some compaines don't offer any beneits only because they're smaller. It could also imply that they're struggling as a company.
Benefits may not be overly important to you, working for a company without beneifts is something to consider. Say that you have an accident at the workplace that doesnt offer you health insurance, you would have to come out of your pocket with money to pay for medical bills that are NOT Cheap!

    Common Job benefits...

  • Health Isurance- This is one of the standard benefits that organizations usually offer full-time employees.

  • Phased Retirement- For workers who plan to retire eventually.

  • Vacation Time- This is one of the more flexible and variable benefits. However, there are often specific guidelines on vacation days

4. The Money

When looking for a job offer, or comparing two, often the most tempting thing to do is to go for the money.

Pros

  • You will be finacially stable. This means you will be able to take care of children, everyday needs, and etc
  • The job matches both your interest and personalitly
  • The opportunities

Cons

  • Money will not guarantee your happiness if the job or boss isn't good.
  • The commute to the workplace may take up most of your paycheck, so going for the money would make ZERO sense.
  • You may not have time for yourself and family because you are working more hours just because your pay is higher.

Creating a Resume

If you follow the steps listed below you would have no trouble completing a Resume

Step 1- Decide which type of Resume you want.

There are three different types of resumes:

Chronological- is the most traditional formal and lifts experiences according to the order in which they took place.
*Appealing to older readers; may be best suited for a conservative field*

Functional- is a type of resume that lists your experiences according to skill.
*Used if you're changing a career direction because it displays your fictional skills first, and work experience.

Combination- is a combination of Chronological and Functional formats.
*Make sure you are paying attention to length !!!*

Step 2- Create a Header

A header shoudld include your name,address with zip code, phone number, and email address.

Step 3- Create a career objective

In one or two sentences, state the job you're attemping to secure in the organization.

Step 4- List your experiences or skills

Starting with your most recent or current job, list your previous work experience.

TIPS:

*This section shows where you have worked and where. It also states specific accomplishments for each position or job*

Example:
Camp Granite Falls, Area Director, Mountainville, TN June 2003 – September 2007 - Directed staff of four while supervising 20 campers. - Taught crafts, sports and cooking.

Step 5- List activities

List activities in which participated and include when your specific role.

TIPS:
*This is the place to note membership or leadership positions in clubs, organizations of any kind, athletic teams, comunition organizations, and so on. *

Step 6- List your Education

List the schools you have attended, starting with the most recent one. Include details such as, GPA, class rank, or special awards.

Step 7 - List your Personal Interest

This section is where you show that you’re a well-rounded person, someone people would want to know and work with. This section is often used by the employer at the start of an interview to break the ice.

Examples:

Ceramics, camping, reading, soccer, automotive repair, carpentry
